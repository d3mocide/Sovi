Brand · Mark Study

Rebalancing the Sovi mark

The current mark tells the right story — debt falling to zero — but it leans left and reads thin. This study diagnoses why, then surfaces six balanced alternatives drawn from Sovi's core promise: privacy, ownership, control.

01

The incumbent

Today's mark is a single sky stroke that descends and flattens onto a baseline, ending in an emerald dot — the product's whole thesis (debt → zero) compressed into one line, set on a rounded navy app-tile.

02

Diagnosis

optical mass pools left of the geometric center

  • 1

    Weighted to the left

    The stroke is dense and tall at the left and trails thin to the right. Its optical center sits well left of middle, so on a square tile the mark looks like it's sliding off-axis.

  • 2

    Reads flat & thin

    One 3px hairline on a dark field has almost no presence. There's no solid silhouette to anchor it — the mark recedes instead of holding the tile.

  • 3

    Fragile when small

    At favicon sizes the curve collapses into an ambiguous squiggle. The emerald dot — the only "event" — is the first thing to disappear.

  • 4

    Story without a symbol

    "Debt to zero" is legible only once explained, and it says nothing about Sovi's louder promise: that your data is yours and stays home.

03

What the new mark must do

BALANCED

Optical weight centered on the vertical axis. Symmetric or center-anchored so it sits square in the tile.

PRESENT

A solid silhouette, not a stray line. It should hold a dark field and survive monochrome.

MEANINGFUL

Carry the brand's core promise — privacy, ownership, the self-hosted stack — not only the payoff metaphor.

RESILIENT

Legible at 16px, pairs cleanly with the Sovi wordmark, and keeps emerald reserved for the single "settled / owned" note.

04

Six directions

Each is built from simple geometry, centered for balance, and shown freestanding and on the navy tile. Sky carries structure; emerald is reserved for the single owned / settled core.
